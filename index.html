Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from other devotionals?

+

This isn't just a devotional—it's a battle plan. Each day includes not just reading and prayer, but specific actions to train your body and mind. It's based on the Templar Rule of Life, designed to forge discipline, not just provide information.

How much time will this take each day?

+

The core challenge takes 15-20 minutes per day: 5 minutes reading the Scripture and reflection, 5-10 minutes in prayer, and 5-10 minutes completing the daily action. Some actions (like fasting or extended prayer) may take longer, but those are occasional.

Do I need to start on a specific day?

+

No! You can start the challenge any day of the week. The 30 days are designed to be consecutive, but you set your own start date. Many warriors choose to start on a Sunday or Monday, but any day works.

What if I miss a day?

+

Don't quit—just pick up where you left off. The challenge is about building discipline over 30 days, not perfection. If you miss a day, simply do that day's content the next day. The goal is progress, not perfection.

Is this only for married men?

+

Not at all! The core 30-day challenge is for all Christian men—single, married, young, old. The bonus prayer guides for wives and children are included for those who are married with families, but they're optional additions, not required content.

What format is the challenge in?

+

The challenge is delivered as a PDF file that you can read on any device—computer, tablet, or phone. You can print it if you prefer a physical copy, or use a digital note-taking app to journal your reflections.
